Easy Sewing Projects for Beginners

Ready to start sewing? These beginner-friendly projects will help you get comfortable with your sewing machine, while also building basic skills that will be beneficial for future projects! Each project comes with step-by-step instructions to guide you through the process.

1.Pillow Cover Project

This simple project is a great way to practice sewing straight lines and working with fabric. Plus, in the end you’ll end up with a beautiful piece of home décor! Materials Needed:
-Fabric of your choice (cotton works best for this), 2 pieces that are 12x12 inches each
-Pins (optional, help keep fabric in place)
-Scissors
-Sewing Machine
-Thread
-Measuring tape or ruler
Instructions:
1. Cut two square pieces of fabric (12x12 inches). Make sure the fabric is straight by measuring the edges.
2. Lay the two pieces of fabric with the right sides facing each other.
3. Pin around the edges of the fabric to hold them in place.
4. Sew along all four sides, leaving a small gap on one side to insert the pillow. Use a straight stitch and sew slowly to ensure the fabric stays aligned.
5. Turn the pillowcase inside out through the opening. Insert your pillow and sew the opening closed using a hand stitch.
That’s it! You now have a pillowcase that was made all by you!

Simple Tote Bag

This beginner project introduces you to basic bag construction. It’s also practical and can be used for your groceries, books, or even as a gift!
Materials Needed:
-Fabric (two pieces, 14x16 inches each for the body of the bag, and 2 pieces that are 4x12 inches for each of the straps)
-Scissors
-Sewing Machine
-Thread
-Pins (optional)
Instructions:
1. Cut out the fabric pieces. The body of the bag should be 14x16 inches, and the straps should be 4x12 inches.
2. Fold the top edges of the body pieces down about 1 inch and sew to create a clean edge.
3. Pin the two body pieces together with the right sides facing each other. Sew along the sides and bottom.
4. For the straps, fold the long edges of each strap in toward the center and sew to create a narrow strap.
5. Attach the straps to the body of the bag, then sew around the entire bag for extra durability.
