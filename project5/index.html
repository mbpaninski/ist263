What is Sewing?

Sewing is the process of stitching different fabrics together using a needle and thread to create an end result. Sewing can be done by hand, or with a machine. Projects on this site are done using mostly machines. Being able to sew opens up a world of possibilities! You can make anything from clothes, to accessories, to home décor. Once you learn the basics, sewing is a gateway to crafting and you can make anything you can imagine!

What do I need to get started?

  1. First step of sewing is to gather tools and materials. Sewing machines can range in price from $150-$300 to buy new. However, second hand sewing machines can work just as well and be more cost effective! You will also need to acquire a good pair of fabric scissors, thread, and fabric. A ruler and seam ripper are good tools to have, but not vital to the process.
  2. Second step is to learn the basics! There are basic things that are needed to get started, such as familiarizing yourself with your machine. Doing tasks such as threading the machine, picking your type of stitch, and sewing a simple seam are all necessary to start a project. Starting small and working your way up to larger projects is a great way to build your skill set!
  3. Don’t be afraid to start simple! Rushing into complex designs wont be good for you or your machine. Starting with easy projects that teach basic skills like measuring fabric and following patterns will be the most beneficial for a beginner.

My Sewing Projects

Mask

During Covid, it was getting difficult to gain access to disposable masks. I decided to start sewing my own. I found a very simple pattern, ordered supplies, and started making them. It took a while to get the pattern the way I wanted it, but eventually I had created my own pattern and even made a child size. I even made a doll size! Making these masks turned into a huge hobby for me, I even sold some. The girl pictured is my cousin Parker and her doll that I made masks for. These were some of the first I made!

Quilted Bag

This quilted bag was one of the first things I ever made. I was taught how to sew and quilt by a past babysitter. This was one of the first things that she ever taught me to make. It was quite a simple pattern, but had some complicated steps. I made it for my cousin for her birthday, I even embroidered her name on it!
